Bookshelf

I love reading books and equally love discussing them and to know more about others bookshelf, so I also get asked around sometimes if I have a list of books I loved the most.

Here are the books which I am currently reading (in the bag), the ones I have read (on the bookshelf) and the ones that I plan to read soon.

I have marked a few books in green that I believe are particularly great, and some in light blue which are really good, and others in blue (which as well are worthy reads).

Books in the bag

  • Why We Sleep : The new science of sleep and dreams.
  • Dhirubhai Ambani : - Against all odds , a story of courage, perseverance and hope.

Bookshelf

  • Zero to One : Notes on startups, or how to build the future.
  • Getting Things Done : The art of stress-free productivity.
  • The Hard Thing About Hard Things : Building a business when there are no easy answers.
  • The Seven Spiritual Laws Of Success : A 1994 self-help, pocket-sized book, not detailed but makes the point.
  • Measure What Matters : The simple idea that drives 10x growth - objectives and key results.
  • How to Win Friends and Influence People : One of the best self help book , if you can read only one book to improve your life, I would recommend this.
  • The Fortune at The Bottom of The Pyramid : shows companies how they could reignite profits and growth by serving the world's five billion poorest people.
  • Shoe Dog : A memoir by the creator of Nike, Phil Knight.
  • Sprint : How to solve big problems and test new ideas in just five days.
  • Business Adventures : Best business book I’ve ever read - Bill Gates
  • Guide to Startups - Pmarca : Marc Andreessen's guide to high tech startups.
  • Wings of Fire : An Autobiography of Dr APJ Abdul Kalam.
